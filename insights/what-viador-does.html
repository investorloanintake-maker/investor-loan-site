The problem Viador was built to solve

Real estate investors in the U.S. are largely underserved by conventional financing. The traditional mortgage system was designed for owner-occupants -- W-2 income, one property, personal DTI. Once an investor owns three properties, qualifications get complicated. By property six or eight, the conventional path has often closed entirely.

Meanwhile, the investor financing market has expanded significantly: DSCR loans, business purpose structures, bridge programs, foreign national programs, second mortgage products on investment properties. But the market is fragmented, lender overlays vary widely, and most investors encounter these products through brokers whose incentive is to close a transaction -- not to help an investor think through capital structure.

Viador exists to fill the advisory gap.

What advisory means here

Viador Partners is a capital-structure advisory platform. Not a mortgage brokerage. The starting point is not "which loan can we close?" -- it is "given where you are as an investor, what does your capital structure actually need to do?"

That question has different answers depending on your strategy, your portfolio stage, your entity structure, your equity position, your hold period, and your next intended move. A cash-flow investor holding ten rentals inside an LLC has a different set of options than a foreign national buying their first U.S. investment property. A fix-and-flip operator scaling volume needs different financing than an equity-rich owner looking to reposition locked capital.

The financing conversation starts with the scenario -- not the rate sheet.

How lending fits in

When the advisory conversation points toward a loan -- and it often does -- all lending is through Focus Home Mortgage Inc. NMLS #2769672. Viador works with Focus Home Mortgage because the product menu covers the full investor financing stack: DSCR, fix and flip, business purpose, bridge, foreign national, and second mortgage on investment property.

The advisory relationship and the lending relationship are distinct. The conversation about which path makes sense comes first. If a transaction follows, it moves through Focus Home Mortgage Inc.

What the conversation looks like

An advisory session is a direct conversation about your investor scenario. Property type. Hold strategy. Entity structure. Portfolio stage. What you are trying to accomplish and what constraints you are working within.

The output is a clear view of which financing paths may fit and why -- before any commitment, before a credit pull, before a loan application. Understanding the options first is the point.

Use the Deal Analyzer or DSCR Calculator to prepare the numbers before the conversation. Or just book directly.
