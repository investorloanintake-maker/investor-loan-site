What is a DSCR loan?

A DSCR loan — Debt Service Coverage Ratio loan — is a type of investment property financing that qualifies borrowers based on the rental income the property generates, not the borrower's personal income. The lender looks at whether the property's monthly rent covers the mortgage payment.

The formula is straightforward: DSCR = Monthly Gross Rent ÷ Monthly PITIA Payment. A DSCR of 1.0 means the property breaks even. Most lenders want to see 1.0 or higher — some programs go as low as 0.75 with compensating factors.

Because income verification is removed from the equation, DSCR loans are ideal for self-employed investors, LLC buyers, and anyone whose tax returns don't accurately reflect their financial strength.

Who DSCR loans are for

  • Self-employed investors whose tax write-offs make their income look lower than it is
  • LLC buyers purchasing in a business entity (required by most DSCR programs)
  • Portfolio investors who've hit the conventional 10-property cap
  • First-time investors who want to qualify based on the deal, not their day job
  • Out-of-state investors buying in markets they don't live in

How DSCR qualification works

The lender orders a rental appraisal (Form 1007) that includes a market rent opinion alongside the standard property appraisal. That market rent figure is what's used in the DSCR calculation — not a lease agreement, not your projected numbers.

The monthly payment used in the denominator includes principal, interest, taxes, insurance, and HOA (PITIA). Run that number through the DSCR formula and you'll know where you stand before you apply.

Typical DSCR loan terms

  • Loan amounts: $100,000 – $3M+ (varies by lender)
  • Max LTV: Up to 80% purchase / 75% cash-out refi
  • Loan terms: 30-year fixed, 5/1 ARM, 7/1 ARM, interest-only options
  • Min credit score: 660 (some lenders 640 with lower LTV)
  • Property types: SFR, 2–4 units, condos, planned unit developments
  • Prepayment: Step-down prepayment penalties common (3-2-1 or 5-4-3-2-1)
  • Reserves: 3–6 months PITIA typically required

DSCR Loan FAQ

Can I use a DSCR loan for a short-term rental / Airbnb?

Yes. Many lenders now offer short-term rental DSCR programs that use AirDNA or similar market data instead of a traditional lease. Rates may be slightly higher and LTV slightly lower, but the programs exist and are growing.

Do I need a lease in place to qualify?

No. The lender uses the appraiser's market rent opinion (Form 1007), not an actual lease. A lease can help if it's above market, but it's not required to qualify.

What if my DSCR is below 1.0?

Some lenders offer no-ratio DSCR or sub-1.0 programs, typically at lower LTV (60–65%). These work for properties in appreciation markets where rent doesn't yet cover payment. Talk to us about your specific scenario.

Are DSCR loans available nationwide?

Yes. Our lender network covers all 50 states. There are no geographic restrictions on where you can buy.
