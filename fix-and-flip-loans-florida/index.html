The 70% Rule — MAO Formula

Maximum Allowable Offer
MAO = ARV × 70% − Rehab Costs
Example: $400K ARV × 70% = $280K − $60K rehab = $220K max purchase price
Built-in margin covers profit (~15%), selling costs (~8–10%), and holding costs (~5–7%).

The 70% rule is the standard underwriting guardrail for flippers and lenders alike. Deals that exceed 70% of ARV compress your margin and leave no room for surprises — permit delays, material cost overruns, or market softening.

How Rehab Draws Work

Most fix and flip lenders fund 100% of the rehab budget, but not upfront. Funds are released in draws as work is completed:

  • Submit scope of work (SOW) — Itemized rehab budget with line items, approved at closing.
  • Complete a phase of work — Kitchen, bathrooms, roof, etc. per SOW line items.
  • Request a draw — Lender sends inspector to verify completed work.
  • Funds released — Typically 3–5 business days after inspection approval.

This means you need cash reserves to front the first phase of rehab before your first draw. Budget $15K–$30K in accessible capital for a typical Florida flip.
