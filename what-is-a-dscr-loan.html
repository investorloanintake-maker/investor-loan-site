What Is a DSCR Loan?

The loan most real estate investors should be using — and most have never heard of. A complete guide from a lender who originates them daily.

Viador Partners, NMLS #2822744 20 Years Lending Experience Florida & Ohio

A DSCR loan — Debt Service Coverage Ratio loan — is an investment property mortgage that qualifies you based on the property's rental income rather than your personal income. No W-2s. No tax returns. No debt-to-income ratio calculation. The only question that matters: does the property generate enough rent to cover the mortgage payment?

DSCR loans are the primary financing tool for serious real estate investors in 2026. If you own rental properties, are self-employed, have exhausted conventional financing limits, or simply prefer not to share years of tax returns with a lender — this is the loan type built for you.

How DSCR Is Calculated

The DSCR formula is simple:

DSCR = Monthly Gross Rent ÷ Monthly PITIA

PITIA = Principal + Interest + Taxes + Insurance + HOA (if applicable). A DSCR of 1.0 means the property breaks even. A DSCR of 1.25 means rent is 25% higher than the payment.

Example: A Tampa rental property generates $2,400/month in rent. The PITIA is $1,850/month. DSCR = 2,400 ÷ 1,850 = 1.30. This property qualifies at most lenders and gets competitive rate pricing.

Who Should Use a DSCR Loan

DSCR loans are ideal for:

  • Self-employed investors — Tax returns show less income than actual cash flow due to deductions. DSCR ignores tax returns entirely.
  • Investors at the conventional limit — Conventional loans cap at 10 financed properties. DSCR has no portfolio cap.
  • LLC investors — DSCR loans close in entity names. Conventional loans require personal vesting.
  • Short-term rental investors — Airbnb and VRBO income qualifies with most DSCR programs.
  • Retirees and wealth-stage investors — Strong net worth but limited W-2 income. DSCR qualifies on the property.
  • Foreign nationals — US conventional loans require US tax history. DSCR programs are available for foreign investors.
  • Fast-moving investors — DSCR closings are typically faster than conventional.

DSCR Loan Requirements

While DSCR loans don't require income documentation, lenders do verify:

  • Credit score: 620 minimum, 660+ for best pricing, 740+ for best rates
  • Down payment: 20–25% for purchase, 25–30% for cash-out refinance
  • Property types: 1–4 unit residential, condos, short-term rentals, 5–8 unit (some programs)
  • Loan amounts: $100,000 to $3M+ depending on lender
  • Reserves: Typically 3–6 months of PITIA in liquid assets
  • Property condition: Standard appraisal required, no major deferred maintenance
  • No recent major derogatory credit: Bankruptcy 2–4 year seasoning, foreclosure 3–7 years

DSCR Loan Rates in 2026

DSCR rates are typically 0.5-1.5% higher than conventional investment property rates. As of 2026, DSCR rates generally range from 6.5% to 8.5% depending on:

  • DSCR ratio — higher DSCR means lower rate
  • LTV — lower LTV means lower rate
  • Credit score — higher score means lower rate
  • Loan term — 30-year fixed, ARM, or interest-only each have different pricing
  • Property type — short-term rentals and condos typically add a small premium
  • Prepayment penalty — accepting a 3- or 5-year prepayment penalty usually lowers the rate

Rate Context

A DSCR loan at 7.5% on a property with strong cash flow is often a better financial decision than a conventional loan at 6.5% on a marginal deal. Rate matters — but cash flow, appreciation, and flexibility matter too. Don't let a rate premium alone prevent you from building a portfolio.

How to Apply for a DSCR Loan

The DSCR loan process is simpler than conventional because income documentation is removed:

  1. Submit the deal

    Property address, purchase price, estimated rent, down payment amount, credit score range. That's enough to get a preliminary assessment.

  2. Receive preliminary terms

    Rate, loan amount, estimated PITIA, and DSCR ratio confirmation — usually within 24 hours of submission.

  3. Formal application

    Credit pull, property details, entity documentation if LLC vesting. No tax returns or income verification at any stage.

  4. Appraisal

    The appraisal includes a market rent analysis confirming the rental income used in the DSCR calculation.

  5. Underwriting and clear to close

    DSCR underwriting is typically faster than conventional. Most deals clear underwriting in 2–3 weeks.

  6. Close

    Close in the name of your LLC if desired. Funds wire, keys transfer, you own the investment.

DSCR stands for Debt Service Coverage Ratio. It is the ratio of a property's monthly rental income to its monthly debt service (mortgage payment plus taxes, insurance, and HOA). A DSCR above 1.0 means the property generates more income than it costs to carry.

Most DSCR lenders require a minimum credit score of 620. Some programs allow scores as low as 600 with lower LTV. A credit score below 620 makes DSCR qualification difficult. Improving your credit before applying typically results in better rates and terms.

Non-QM does not mean risky -- it means the loan uses alternative qualification criteria. DSCR is one of the most common and well-established non-QM products.

DSCR loan amounts typically range from $100,000 to $3M+ depending on the lender. For larger amounts, some lenders offer jumbo DSCR programs up to $5M. The maximum loan amount depends on property value, DSCR ratio, and LTV.

Yes. Both rate-and-term refinances and cash-out refinances are available with DSCR loans. Cash-out DSCR refinances typically allow up to 75-80% LTV with a 6-month seasoning requirement.

The loan application will result in a credit inquiry, but if the loan closes in an LLC name, the ongoing mortgage may or may not report to personal credit bureaus depending on the lender. Ask about this specifically if it matters to your situation.
