Investor financing questions, answered

Getting Started

Do I need experience to get an investor loan?

Many investor loan programs allow first-time investors depending on the property, structure, and overall scenario. A strong deal with a solid down payment often matters more than experience. Submit your deal and we'll tell you what's realistic.

Do I need W-2 income to qualify for a DSCR loan?

No — DSCR loans qualify based on the property's rental income relative to the loan payment, not your personal income. This makes them useful for self-employed investors, business owners, and anyone whose income doesn't fit traditional documentation requirements.

What is a DSCR loan and how does it work?

DSCR stands for Debt Service Coverage Ratio. It measures whether a rental property's income covers its loan payment. A DSCR of 1.0 means rent equals the payment; above 1.25 is typically what lenders prefer. Use our DSCR Quick Check to estimate yours.

Can I get a fix and flip loan with no experience?

Some fix and flip programs do work with first-time investors, particularly when the deal has a strong equity cushion and a realistic rehab scope. The deal has to make sense on paper. Use our Fix & Flip Analyzer to check your numbers first.

Down Payments & Qualifying

How much down payment is required for an investment property?

For rental property DSCR loans, expect 20–25% down as a starting point, depending on the lender and property type. Fix and flip loans are structured differently — they typically lend on a percentage of cost or ARV rather than a fixed down payment. The right structure depends on your deal.

Can I get an investor loan with a 680 credit score?

Credit score requirements vary by program and lender. Some DSCR programs go down to 640–660; others require 700+. A lower score often means a higher rate or larger down payment. The property and deal structure matter alongside credit.

Does the property need to be already rented to qualify for a DSCR loan?

Not always. Many lenders use a market rent estimate (often supported by an appraisal) to calculate DSCR rather than requiring an existing lease. You can often qualify before a tenant is in place.

Can I use a DSCR loan for a short-term rental (Airbnb, VRBO)?

Some lenders will consider short-term rental income for DSCR calculations, but guidelines vary significantly. Some require a history of rental income; others use projections. This is a specific scenario worth reviewing on a deal-by-deal basis.

Loan Types & Programs

What types of properties do you work with?

Single family, duplexes, triplexes, fourplexes, condos, townhomes, and mixed-use. Fix & flip, buy-and-hold rentals, and refinances all fit within the advisory model. We'll let you know upfront if a scenario falls outside typical program guidelines.

What's the difference between a DSCR loan and a conventional investment loan?

A conventional investment loan qualifies you using personal income, DTI ratio, and employment history. A DSCR loan qualifies you primarily using the property's rental income. DSCR is typically faster, requires less documentation, and works well for self-employed investors or those with multiple properties.

Can I do a cash-out refinance on an investment property I already own?

Yes — many investors use cash-out refinances to pull equity from existing rentals and reinvest it into new deals. Qualifying depends on the property's value, existing loan balance, rental income, and your overall profile.

Do you work with investors buying 5+ unit multifamily properties?

Properties with 5 or more units follow different lending guidelines than 1–4 unit residential investment properties. Submit your deal and we'll assess which path makes sense for your scenario.
