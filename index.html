Investor financing questions, answered

Business Purpose Loans (BPL)

What is a Business Purpose Loan (BPL)?

A Business Purpose Loan is a real estate loan made to a business entity — typically an LLC — for an investment property. Unlike conventional loans, BPL is underwritten on the deal and the asset, not your personal income. It falls outside consumer lending regulations, which removes the property count limits and income documentation requirements that cap most investors. Read our full BPL guide →

How is BPL different from a DSCR loan?

DSCR is a type of BPL — it's a specific underwriting method (rental income vs. debt payment). BPL is the broader framework. Under BPL you can also structure fix and flip loans, bridge loans, and blanket portfolio loans — things a DSCR model doesn't cover. The key is that both require an entity borrower and business purpose use.

Do I need an LLC to get a BPL loan?

Yes. Business Purpose Loans require a business entity as the borrower — most commonly an LLC. If you don't have one, it's a straightforward and inexpensive setup (typically under $200 in Florida or Ohio). We can point you in the right direction.

Does BPL have a property count limit?

No — that's one of the main reasons investors use it. Conventional Fannie/Freddie loans cap at 10 per borrower. Since BPL is entity-based (your LLC borrows, not you personally), there is no equivalent ceiling.

Getting Started

Do I need experience to get an investor loan?

Many investor loan programs allow first-time investors depending on the property, structure, and overall scenario. A strong deal with a solid down payment often matters more than experience. Submit your deal and we'll tell you what's realistic.

Do I need W-2 income to qualify for a DSCR loan?

No — DSCR loans qualify based on the property's rental income relative to the loan payment, not your personal income. This makes them useful for self-employed investors, business owners, and anyone whose income doesn't fit traditional documentation requirements.

Can I get a fix and flip loan with no experience?

Some fix and flip programs do work with first-time investors, particularly when the deal has a strong equity cushion and a realistic rehab scope. The deal has to make sense on paper. Use our Fix & Flip Analyzer to check your numbers first.

Down Payments & Qualifying

How much down payment is required?

For DSCR loans, expect 20–25% down as a starting point. Fix and flip loans are structured differently — typically lending on a percentage of cost or ARV. BPL and bridge loans vary by deal. Submit your scenario and we'll give you realistic guidance.

Can I get an investor loan with a 680 credit score?

Credit score requirements vary by program and lender. Some DSCR and BPL programs go down to 620–640; others require 700+. A lower score often means a slightly higher rate. The property and deal structure carry significant weight alongside credit.

Can I use a DSCR or BPL loan for a short-term rental?

Some lenders will consider short-term rental income (Airbnb, VRBO) for DSCR and BPL calculations, but guidelines vary. This is a specific scenario worth reviewing deal-by-deal — submit your property and we'll assess what's possible.
