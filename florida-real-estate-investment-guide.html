Florida Real Estate Investment Guide 2026

The full picture on Florida investing — which markets still cash flow, where the appreciation plays are, and how DSCR financing makes it work without W-2s.

Viador Partners, NMLS #2822744 20 Years Lending Experience Viador Partners LLC

Florida has been one of the most active real estate investment states in the country for the past decade. Strong population growth, no state income tax, a diversified economy across tourism, finance, tech, and healthcare, and some of the most active short-term rental markets in the world have drawn investors from every corner of the country. The market has matured significantly since 2020. Deals that were easy to find in 2019 require more creativity today. But Florida still offers exceptional investment opportunities — they just require more market knowledge, better financing, and sharper deal analysis.

Florida's Investment Landscape in 2026

Florida's investment market has evolved significantly since the pandemic-era buying frenzy:

The fundamentals remain strong: Florida continues to attract net in-migration from high-tax northeastern and western states, the employment base is diversifying beyond tourism, and rental demand in virtually every major metro remains above supply. The challenge is that insurance costs have increased dramatically, prices in core markets have appreciated significantly, and the era of easily positive DSCR deals in Tampa Bay and Orlando is largely over for buy-and-hold investors at 20% down.

The investors winning in 2026 Florida are finding deals in the second-tier markets, using STR income underwriting where it applies, and managing insurance costs aggressively.

Florida Market-by-Market Guide

Florida's investment markets are not monolithic. Each major metro has a distinct profile:

  • Tampa Bay (Hillsborough, Pinellas, Pasco) — Strongest employment base, most balanced market. Core Tampa and St. Pete have compressed DSCR ratios due to appreciation and insurance. Pasco County and East Hillsborough remain positive cash flow. STR market active on Gulf Coast. Median SFR ~$385K.
  • Orlando/Kissimmee — Largest STR market in the country (Disney corridor). Long-term rental market driven by massive employment growth. More affordable than Tampa Bay core. Strong for both STR DSCR and long-term rental hold. Median SFR ~$340K.
  • Jacksonville — Most affordable major Florida metro. Military, logistics, and healthcare employment base. Strong positive cash flow at 25% down in most submarkets. Lowest insurance costs of major Florida cities. Median SFR ~$290K.
  • South Florida (Miami, Fort Lauderdale, West Palm Beach) — Premium market, tight DSCR ratios on long-term rent, active STR and foreign national investor market. Highest insurance costs. Best for appreciation-focused investors. Median SFR $425-600K.
  • Southwest Florida (Naples, Fort Myers, Sarasota) — Luxury vacation rental and seasonal market. Strong STR income potential. Significant insurance exposure (Ian impact). Premium acquisition costs. Median SFR $450-650K.
  • Panhandle (Pensacola, Panama City Beach, Destin/30A) — Exceptional STR market, more affordable than South Florida, military employment anchor in Pensacola. Strong seasonal demand. Median SFR $280-400K.

Where Florida Cash Flow Still Works in 2026

The markets and property types still producing positive DSCR at 25% down in 2026:

  • Pasco County — Wesley Chapel, Land O Lakes. Lower prices, lower insurance than coastal, strong rental demand from Tampa spillover. DSCR 1.05-1.20 achievable.
  • East Hillsborough — Riverview, Brandon. Established rental market, family tenants, more affordable than Tampa proper. DSCR 1.00-1.15 achievable.
  • Polk County — Lakeland, Winter Haven. Highest cap rates in the Tampa metro area. Growing market. DSCR 1.10-1.30 achievable.
  • Jacksonville — Most submarkets still produce positive DSCR. Lowest Florida insurance costs. Best long-term rental fundamentals of any Florida major metro at current prices.
  • STR properties statewide — Properties in tourist markets where STR income is 2x+ long-term rent. Orlando Kissimmee corridor, Panhandle beaches, and select Gulf Coast markets.

DSCR Financing for Florida Investment Properties

DSCR loans are the primary financing tool for serious Florida investors:

  • No income documentation — critical as Florida investors often have significant depreciation write-offs
  • LLC vesting — Florida LLC formation is quick and inexpensive; DSCR closes in entity names
  • STR programs — Florida's exceptional STR market is supported by DSCR lenders with specific Airbnb income underwriting
  • Insurance management — work with a lender who understands Florida insurance dynamics and calculates real PITIA, not estimates
  • No portfolio cap — scale without the 10-property conventional limit
