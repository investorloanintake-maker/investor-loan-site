DSCR Calculator

Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) is a rental property's gross rent divided by its monthly housing payment (PITIA). This tool estimates DSCR from the figures you enter — no rates, no qualification decision. Viador Partners is an investor-financing advisory and is not the lender.

By Chad Evers, Mortgage Loan Originator, NMLS #2822744 · Last reviewed: June 8, 2026

Estimated DSCR
—

Enter monthly gross rent and the property's monthly PITIA components, then select Calculate. DSCR = gross rent ÷ PITIA.

This is an educational estimate, not a Loan Estimate, an offer, or a commitment to lend. Actual terms are set by the lender and program and depend on your full profile. Lender overlays vary.

Methodology

Formula. PITIA = principal & interest + taxes + insurance + HOA/association dues. DSCR = monthly gross rent / PITIA, rounded to two decimals. A DSCR of 1.00 means the rent exactly covers the housing payment.

Interpretation (educational, not an approval signal). Many investor lenders look for a DSCR around 1.0–1.25, but minimums, rent treatment (especially for short-term rentals), reserves, LTV, FICO, property type, and prepayment structure vary materially by lender and program. This tool does not make a qualification decision.

Alternative definitions. Some lenders and commercial analyses use a net-operating-income (NOI) based DSCR (NOI ÷ debt service) rather than gross-rent ÷ PITIA. This calculator uses the common residential gross-rent ÷ PITIA convention. Lender overlays vary; this is not a qualification decision.

Hypothetical (not an offer). If a property had $3,000 monthly gross rent and $2,500 total PITIA, the DSCR would be 1.20. This illustrates the math only — it is not a quote, rate, or offer.

A note from a licensed MLO

DSCR is a fast way to see where a deal stands, but the ratio alone doesn't decide anything — how a lender treats the rent (especially short-term rental), reserves, LTV, and credit all move the outcome.
